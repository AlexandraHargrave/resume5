My job title is business administration, and I am proud to say that this job is much deeper and has much more depth than one may think. You work inside and outside of the office, working with all different types of people, every age, and all different walks of life.

In this position, it is necessary to have a background, and some experience in leadership, sportmanship and dedication. Although this may seem like an easy self explanitory job, there is much more depth and growth that comes with this position. Because a lot of the work done in this field is self involving, self taught, and self discovered, one must have the drive to find work that makes a larger impact on their owm communities.

Some of the things I do in a day are meetings, after meetings, after meetings. I also talk and meet with groups and individuals throughout the community and throughout our workplace. We help and try to create plans that will enable the people within a community to find valuable work that will directly help their communities. Aside from meetings, I also deal with a lot that goes on in the workplace. By this I mean, I have to make sure everything in the business runs smoothly, and the customers, whomever they may be, are satisfied. While this job comes with its challenges, there are also many rewarding moments as well. Some of which are seeing the people of your very own community happy, finally feeling as if they have a purpose and finally able to support their families. It's seeing POC women prospering in fields dominated by men. Seeing children expressing how proud they are of their parents or sisters and brothers. Althought the title "Business Administration" seems dull and not exciting, it is the complete opposite.
